Difference between Padding, Margins and Borders

Padding Margins Borders
The inner space between the content and the border of your box. The outer space (or lack of space) surrounding the box. The perimeter of the box. Borders can be invisible or they could be a thick colored line like the green one pictured above.
Padding controls how much breathing room exists within a box Margin controls how much breathing room or whitespace exists outside of a box. Border is between the margin and padding of an object. Only counted when given an actual width otherwise, it’s zero.
Padding can be set to zero pixels and above. It can change its own element's size and impact how much of a fill color or background image is visible within a box. Margins, on the other hand, are essentially invisible barriers around a box. They define how an element interacts with adjacent elements or the edge of a screen -
